gAI & the Environment

Spin the fish. Hover and click on glowing areas to learn more.

2020: Pre-gAI Baseline

Why a Betta Fish?

The betta is solitary and fragile—just one fish per tank. That mirrors humanity’s single planet: no backup world to move to. Its vivid colors show the beauty and irreplaceability of Earth’s resources. As colors fade and scales drift away, the fish becomes a living metaphor for gAI’s environmental cost: progress means nothing without the ecosystem that sustains it.

Why a Fish at All?

Fish are living indicators of ecosystem health—water quality, temperature, pollution, biodiversity loss. When the environment suffers, they show the damage first. In water, changes in clarity, color, and movement feel immediate. Watching the fish deteriorate makes the impact visible and emotional, not just a number.

How to use this

  • Drag the timeline to change years; watch the fish, scales, particles, and facts update.
  • Click the glowing scale flares on the fish to toggle their fact callouts; click again to hide.
  • Hover/click the glowing hitboxes to read environmental impacts.
  • Orbit: drag to rotate, scroll/pinch to zoom—controls stay centered on the fish.

How this was made

  • Three.js scene with HDR-ish lighting and ACES filmic tonemapping.
  • GLTF betta model + built-in skeletal animation; additive halo + rim lights.
  • Timeline slider drives pollution progress, tint, fog, particles, and scale “flares.”
  • Canvas blueprint overlay and CSS animated underwater layers/particles.
  • Tooltip callouts follow scale flares; HUD is glassy, pointer-safe UI.
